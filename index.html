The rugged terrains of Arunachal Pradesh

The rugged terrains of Arunachal Pradesh attract adventure seekers from around the world. Trekking, mountaineering, river rafting, angling, and paragliding are popular activities in the region. Moreover, it is home to several revered monasteries and spiritual sites.

Arunachal Pradesh – One of the seven sisters.

Arunachal Pradesh – One of the seven sisters, Arunachal Pradesh is known as the treasure trove of nature's divine beauty. Home to gorgeous orchids and the most elegantly beautiful blooms, Arunachal Pradesh is decked with ancient monasteries and gumphas. The wild rivers, impenetrable forests, endemic bird and animal species, over 500 species of orchids, they all make Arunachal Pradesh a natural wonderland. Arunachal Pradesh has among the highest diversity of mammals and birds in India. There are around 750 species of birds and more than 200 species of mammals in the state.

Rare species of orchids.

Arunachal Pradesh is famous for rare species of orchids, which are found in all types of forests, altitudinal ranges, and rainfall conditions. The many unspoilt tree-clad slopes contain tigers, leopards, elephants, deers, bears and apes. The Mithun, or 'bos frontalis' exists both in wild and semi-domesticated form.

Pakke Tiger Reserve

Pakke Tiger Reserve, is a Project Tiger reserve in the East Kameng district of Arunachal Pradesh in Northeast India. The 862 km2 (333 sq mi) reserve is protected by the Department of Environment and Forest of Arunachal Pradesh.

Snow leopards

In Arunachal Pradesh, snow leopards inhabit the higher Himalayan and trans-Himalayan region at an altitude between 3,000-5,400 metres.
